The Glasgow
Times

Subscription marketing design and the creative direction of iconic community publications for Glasgow's home-grown daily newspaper.

Overview

The Glasgow Times is the city's daily newspaper with a deeply local identity. The design challenge here was dual: creating slick, conversion-driven subscription marketing assets — while also spearheading the visual design of The Wee Red Book, one of Scottish football's most beloved annual publications.

Subscription campaigns targeted Glasgow's loyal readership with direct, community-led messaging. The Wee Red Book required an entirely different gear — a print publication with its own heritage, personality, and fierce fan-base expectations.

Challenge

The Glasgow Times needed subscription campaigns that cut through in a crowded digital environment and spoke authentically to a local Glasgow audience. Simultaneously, the Wee Red Book — a deeply beloved football annual with decades of heritage — required a redesign that modernised its visual identity without alienating a fiercely loyal fanbase.

Strategy

For subscription campaigns, I leaned into Glasgow's identity: direct, no-nonsense messaging paired with bold, warm typography that felt genuinely of the city. For the Wee Red Book, the strategy was evolutionary, not revolutionary — modernising layout systems and introducing cleaner grid structures while preserving the red-and-white colour heritage and the annual's characterful editorial voice.

Execution

Subscription assets were developed as cohesive multi-format families — digital display units, email headers, and in-paper promotional pages — all sharing a common visual language. The Ninja Comp campaign introduced gamification-style design elements to drive competition entries. The Wee Red Book was designed from scratch each season, incorporating editorial photography, squad statistics, and match schedules within a tight, reader-friendly print layout.

Results

The Wee Red Book sold out within days of publication in multiple consecutive seasons, with readers citing the updated design as a significant improvement on previous editions. Subscription campaign assets drove measurable uplift in new reader sign-ups. The Ninja Comp campaign delivered strong competition entries and positive reader feedback.
