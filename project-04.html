Impactful editorial graphics, election data visualisations, and subscription campaign design for Scotland's pro-independence daily newspaper.

Overview

The National is Scotland's only daily newspaper to advocate for Scottish independence — a title with a passionate, politically-engaged readership and distinctive visual identity built around its black masthead and bold editorial voice.

My work spanned subscription campaign design, major news event graphics, and the creation of election data visualisations for the Scottish Parliament elections — translating complex political data into clear, engaging visual stories for a digital-first audience.

Challenge

The National's readership is politically engaged, design-literate, and highly opinionated. Subscription campaigns needed to feel urgent without being reductive; news graphics needed to be both accurate and immediately readable. The Scottish Parliament election visualisations in particular required turning raw data — constituency results, seat counts, vote-share shifts — into graphics that respected complexity while enabling rapid comprehension.

Strategy

I worked closely with editorial teams to understand the story behind each data set before designing its visual form. For election coverage, I developed a colour system mapped to party identity that could operate across multiple graphic types — bar charts, constituency maps, and seat-count infographics. For subscription campaigns, I used The National's own bold masthead character as a design element to reinforce brand pride and identity in every asset.

Execution

Election night graphics were produced to tight deadlines as results came in, requiring a pre-built template system that could be populated quickly without sacrificing quality. Student and Black Friday subscription campaigns employed bold, offer-led design with urgency-driven typography. The Salmond trial coverage required a more restrained, journalistically serious visual tone — reflecting the gravity of the story.

Results

Election night visualisations were praised by editorial leadership for their clarity and speed of delivery under deadline pressure. Student subscription campaigns achieved strong conversion rates among younger demographic cohorts. The visual identity system developed for election coverage has been adapted for subsequent major Scottish political events.
