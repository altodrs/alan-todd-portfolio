The Scottish

High-impact subscription campaigns and commercial brand collaborations for Scotland's leading agricultural title.

Overview

The Scottish Farmer is Scotland's longest-running and most widely-read farming publication. As lead designer, I was responsible for the full visual output — from weekly print layouts to multi-channel digital campaigns and commercial partnership assets.

A flagship challenge was the Ardbeg × Scottish Farmer collaboration — a premium whisky brand requiring an editorial aesthetic that felt native to the publication while elevating the advertiser's premium positioning.

Challenge

To design subscription and commercial advertising assets that maintained The Scottish Farmer's editorial integrity while accommodating diverse commercial partners — from premium whisky brands like Ardbeg to lifestyle clothing and seasonal promotions. Each campaign needed to feel bespoke without breaking the parent brand's visual consistency.

Strategy

I established a modular design framework using the publication's masthead typography and a warm editorial colour palette as the constant, while allowing flexibility in photography style and layout structure per campaign. This created a visual family that read as unified yet offered each partner a distinctive voice.

Execution

Assets ranged from full-page print ads and direct mail to digital display banners, email headers, and social graphics. For the Ardbeg collaboration, I developed a premium dark editorial aesthetic — rich photography, understated gold typography, and texture-led layouts that felt at home in both print and on-screen environments. All mockups were produced for client sign-off at every stage.

Results

Subscription campaign assets consistently outperformed previous benchmarks for click-through engagement. Commercial partners including Ardbeg renewed their partnership arrangements following successful campaign activations. The modular framework has continued to serve the title across subsequent campaigns, delivering consistent quality at scale.
